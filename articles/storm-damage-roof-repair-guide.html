How Storm Damage Affects Your Roof and What to Do Next

When severe weather strikes the Twin Cities, your roof bears the brunt of nature's fury. Golf ball-sized hail, winds exceeding 70 mph, and torrential downpours can inflict serious damage that compromises your home's protection. Many homeowners find themselves overwhelmed afterward—uncertain about what occurred, what requires attention, or how to navigate insurance processes.

Storm damage doesn't always show up as obvious leaks or missing shingles. It often hides in ways you wouldn't expect: granule loss that ages your roof faster, tiny cracks that let water seep in, and damaged flashing that won't fail until the next big storm. Spotting these hidden problems early protects your home and saves you money.

Types of Storm Damage That Affect Your Roof

Hail Damage: The Silent Destroyer

Hail damage depends on the size of the stones, wind speed, and how they hit your roof. Small hailstones chip away at protective granules over time, while large ones crack shingles completely, dent gutters, and sometimes punch right through roofing materials.

Wind Damage: More Than Missing Shingles

Strong winds attack roofs from multiple angles. While missing or torn shingles are easy to spot, wind also creates powerful lifting forces that gradually loosen nails, crack seal strips, and stress your roofing system in ways you can't see.

Water Damage: The Progressive Threat

Heavy rain puts every part of your roofing system to the test. A healthy roof channels water away without issue, but damaged spots let water sneak inside—and once it's in, water can travel surprisingly far from where it entered.

Immediate Steps After Storm Damage

Safety First: Assessing Damage Safely

Don't climb on your roof after storm damage—what looks like minor damage can make conditions dangerous. Check your property from the ground using binoculars, or walk around looking for obvious problems.

Temporary Protection Measures

When damage lets water in, temporary protection prevents more interior damage while you arrange real repairs.

Documenting Storm Damage for Insurance Claims

Photography and Evidence Collection

Insurance companies need thorough documentation to process claims smoothly. The more evidence you provide, the easier your claim becomes.

Hidden Damage: What You Can't See

Thermal Imaging and Advanced Detection

Modern damage assessment goes beyond what you can see with your eyes. Thermal imaging cameras pick up moisture problems, insulation issues, and structural damage that regular inspections completely miss.

Drone Inspection Advantages

Drones have changed roof inspection by providing safe, complete views of damage without putting anyone at risk. High-resolution cameras capture details you can't see from the ground.
