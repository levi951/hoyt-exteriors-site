Exterior Home Remodel: Where to Start and How to Plan Your Project

Your home's exterior tells a story the moment someone pulls into your driveway. After years of weather, that story might not be the one you want to tell. Aging siding, a roof that's seen better days, or windows that have lost their charm can leave your home looking tired and outdated.

Planning an exterior remodel doesn't have to feel overwhelming. The secret lies in approaching it strategically rather than trying to tackle everything at once. A thoughtful plan saves money, prevents headaches, and ensures all your improvements work together seamlessly.

Assess Your Home's Current Condition

Start with a Visual Inspection

Look for the obvious problems first. Missing or curling roof shingles, cracked siding, windows that stick or let in drafts, gutters pulling away from the house. Take photos of everything that catches your eye.

Consider Professional Inspections

A professional inspection might reveal moisture damage behind siding, structural concerns, or insulation problems that could derail your renovation plans if discovered mid-project.

Prioritize Projects by Necessity and Impact

Address Safety and Structural Issues First

A leaking roof trumps new paint colors every time. If water is getting where it shouldn't, that's your top priority. Damaged siding that lets moisture reach your home's structure needs attention before it causes expensive interior damage.

Consider Return on Investment

New siding typically recoups 75-85% of its cost at resale, while roof replacement returns 60-70%. But don't let ROI drive every decision. If you're staying put for the long haul, prioritize the improvements that make you happiest.

Set a Realistic Budget

Exterior remodels can cost anywhere from a few thousand for minor updates to $50,000+ for complete makeovers. Nailing down your budget early keeps you from getting carried away.

Budget an extra 15-20% for discoveries like rotted framing, outdated wiring, or drainage issues that only become visible once work begins.

Choose Materials That Work Together

Consider Your Home's Style

A Victorian cottage needs different materials than a sleek contemporary home. Traditional homes often shine with classic materials like wood or fiber cement siding, while modern designs can handle bolder colors and mixed textures.

Coordinate Colors and Textures

Your roof, siding, trim, and accents should feel like they belong together. Start with your roof color — it's usually the most expensive element to change — then choose siding and trim colors that complement it.

Plan for Energy Efficiency

Modern exterior improvements offer real opportunities to cut energy costs. Many upgrades qualify for rebates or tax credits that help offset the initial investment.

Navigate the Permitting Process

Most exterior improvements require permits, and approval can take weeks or months. Start this process early to avoid project delays.

Select the Right Contractor

Your contractor choice can make or break your exterior remodel. Look for professionals with the right experience, credentials, and approach for your specific project. Working with a company that handles roofing, siding, windows, and other exterior elements streamlines communication.

Time Your Project Strategically

Spring and summer are peak seasons for exterior work, meaning higher prices and longer waits for quality contractors. Fall often offers good weather and better availability.

Prepare Your Property and Household

Move outdoor furniture, cover plants near work areas, and protect landscaping. Remove pictures and mirrors from exterior walls since the constant work creates vibrations.

Monitor Progress and Quality

Stay involved throughout your project without hovering over workers. Regular check-ins help catch issues early and ensure work meets your expectations.
