What to Expect During a Roof Replacement: A Day-by-Day Breakdown

You've signed the contract. The materials are ordered. Your roof replacement is scheduled to start Monday morning in Apple Valley, and you're wondering what exactly happens next.

Most contractors leave you guessing about the process. They show up, make noise, and disappear without explanation. That's not how we work at Hoyt Exteriors. After 25+ years of roof replacements across the Twin Cities metro, we know that informed homeowners are happier homeowners.

Here's exactly what to expect during your roof replacement, day by day.

Before the First Day: Pre-Project Preparation

The Week Before Installation

Your project manager contacts you to confirm the start date and weather contingency plans. Minnesota weather doesn't follow schedules, so we build flexibility into every timeline.

We deliver materials 1-2 days before installation begins. Shingles, underlayment, flashing, and ventilation components arrive on pallets.

Day 1: Tear-Off and Preparation

7:00 AM - Crew Arrival and Setup

Our GAF Master Elite certified crew arrives with a full truck of equipment. They set up safety barriers, lay protective tarps over landscaping, and position dumpsters for debris removal.

8:00 AM - Drone Pre-Inspection

Before anyone climbs on your roof, we fly our drone for a final inspection. This documents existing conditions and identifies any last-minute issues the crew needs to address.

9:00 AM - Tear-Off Begins

This is the loudest part of your roof replacement. The crew systematically removes old shingles, starting from the ridge and working down. They use specialized tools to minimize damage to decking underneath.

Day 2: Installation Foundation

7:30 AM - Weather Check and Setup

Minnesota weather changes fast. The crew checks conditions and confirms the day's plan. Light rain stops work. Wind over 25 mph stops work. Your safety and quality matter more than schedule pressure.

8:00 AM - Underlayment Installation

The crew installs synthetic underlayment across the entire roof deck. This waterproof barrier protects your home even before shingles go on.

Day 3: Shingle Installation

7:30 AM - Final Material Staging

The crew positions shingle bundles across the roof for efficient installation. Proper staging prevents unnecessary trips up and down ladders, speeding installation and improving safety.

8:00 AM - Shingle Installation Begins

Starting from the bottom edge, the crew installs shingles in overlapping courses. Each shingle gets nailed according to manufacturer specifications – typically 4-6 nails per shingle, placed in the nail zone.

Day 4: Final Details and Cleanup

8:00 AM - Final Inspection Walk

The crew lead conducts a complete roof inspection, checking nail patterns, shingle alignment, and flashing details. Any issues get corrected immediately.

11:00 AM - Final Drone Documentation

We fly our drone again to document the completed installation. This creates a permanent record of your new roof's condition and provides baseline documentation for warranty purposes.
