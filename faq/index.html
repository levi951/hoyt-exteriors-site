Technology

How does drone inspection improve roof lifespan?

+

Our AI-powered drones capture high-resolution imagery of every square foot of your roof, including areas unsafe to walk. Combined with thermal imaging, we detect moisture intrusion, insulation failures, and flashing problems invisible to ground-level inspection. Early detection means repairs happen before small issues become expensive failures — extending your roof’s effective lifespan by catching problems years earlier. Per InterNACHI standards, infrared inspection is the most reliable method for non-invasive moisture detection.

What is thermal imaging and why does it matter?

+

Thermal imaging uses infrared cameras to detect temperature differences across your building’s exterior. These temperature variations reveal hidden moisture trapped under roofing membranes, insulation gaps, air leaks around windows, and failing flashing — all invisible to the naked eye. According to the U.S. Department of Energy, thermographic inspections can identify areas of missing or damaged insulation in a fraction of the time of traditional methods.

Siding

What siding materials does Hoyt Exteriors install?

+

We install vinyl siding, fiber cement (James Hardie), engineered wood (LP SmartSide), premium wood siding, and metal panel systems. Each material is matched to your home’s performance requirements, aesthetic goals, and budget.

How long does siding last in Minnesota?

+

Siding lifespan varies by material: vinyl lasts 20–40 years, fiber cement 30–50 years, LP SmartSide comes with a 50-year warranty, and metal panel can exceed 50 years. Minnesota’s freeze-thaw cycles are the primary factor — proper installation with correct moisture barriers is critical.

Windows

How much can new windows save on energy bills?

+

According to the EPA ENERGY STAR program, certified windows can save homeowners $126–$465 per year on energy bills, depending on the type of windows being replaced. In Minnesota’s climate, the savings trend toward the higher end due to extreme winter temperatures.

What U-factor should windows have for Minnesota?

+

For Minnesota (Climate Zone 6/7), ENERGY STAR recommends windows with a U-factor of 0.25 or lower. Triple-pane windows with U-factors below 0.20 are ideal for our climate and provide the best energy performance.

Insulation

What R-value does Minnesota code require for attic insulation?

+

Minnesota falls in IECC Climate Zones 6 and 7, which require attic insulation of R-49 to R-60. Many older Minnesota homes have only R-19 to R-30, which is well below current code requirements.

How much can insulation save on energy costs?

+

According to the U.S. Department of Energy, homeowners can save an average of 15% on heating and cooling costs — and up to 20% or more in northern climates like Minnesota — by air sealing and adding insulation to attics and basement rim joists.
