WINDOW MATERIALS

Four Materials, One Standard

Every window we install meets the highest performance standards. Choose the frame material that fits your home's style, performance needs, and budget.

Vinyl Windows

The most popular choice for Twin Cities homeowners. Vinyl frames deliver excellent energy efficiency at an affordable price point, with virtually zero maintenance required.

  • Most affordable option — best value per dollar
  • Multi-chamber frames for superior insulation
  • Never needs painting, staining, or sealing
  • Wide range of styles and configurations

ENERGY STAR-certified windows can save homeowners $126–$465 per year on energy bills, depending on the windows being replaced.

Fiberglass Windows

The strongest frames in the industry with the best insulation values. Fiberglass expands and contracts at nearly the same rate as glass, maintaining a perfect seal through Minnesota's temperature swings.

  • Strongest frame material available
  • Best insulation of any frame type
  • Expands/contracts with glass — maintains seal
  • Paintable for custom color matching

Per the U.S. Department of Energy, heat gain and loss through windows accounts for 25–30% of residential heating and cooling energy use.

Wood Windows

Classic beauty that has stood the test of time. Wood frames offer excellent natural insulation and an unmatched warmth that complements both traditional and modern home styles.

  • Classic aesthetic — warm, natural beauty
  • Excellent natural insulation properties
  • Can be stained or painted any color
  • Ideal for historic homes and upscale renovations

Composite Windows

The best of all worlds — composite frames combine wood fibers with polymer resins to create windows that look like wood, perform like fiberglass, and maintain like vinyl.

  • Combines strength, beauty, and low maintenance
  • Won't rot, warp, or crack
  • Eco-friendly — made from reclaimed materials
  • Premium performance at a mid-range price
ENERGY EFFICIENCY

Stop Heating the Outdoors

In Minnesota, your windows are the frontline against -20°F winters and 95°F summers. Old or failing windows can account for a significant portion of your home's energy loss. Modern window technology changes everything.

Low-E Glass Coatings

Microscopically thin metallic layers reflect radiant heat back into your home in winter and block solar heat gain in summer.

Argon Gas Fill

Dense, inert argon gas between panes provides far better insulation than air alone — reducing heat transfer and condensation.

Triple-Pane Option

For maximum performance, triple-pane glass adds another insulating barrier — ideal for north-facing walls and bedrooms.

Warm-Edge Spacers

Non-conductive spacers between panes eliminate the cold spots and condensation common with older aluminum spacers.
