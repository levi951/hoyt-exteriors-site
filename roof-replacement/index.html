Our Process

Roof Replacement, Step by Step

A proven process refined over 25+ years — designed to minimize disruption and maximize the performance of your new roof.

Detailed Evaluation

We begin with a comprehensive inspection of your existing roof — including drone aerial assessment and thermal imaging to identify hidden damage. We evaluate the deck structure, ventilation, flashing, and overall condition to determine exactly what your property needs.

Customized Recommendations

Based on your roof's condition, your home's architecture, local weather patterns, and your budget, we present material options with clear pros and cons. You'll see samples, understand warranty differences, and make an informed choice — no pressure, no upselling.

Professional Tear-Off

Our crew carefully removes all existing roofing materials, inspects the deck for damage or rot, and makes any necessary structural repairs. We protect your landscaping, siding, and windows with tarps and magnetic nail sweeps to keep your property pristine.

Complete Installation

Your new roof is installed to exact manufacturer specifications by our certified technicians. Every component — underlayment, ice-and-water shield, flashing, ventilation, and finish material — is installed with precision and verified through quality checkpoints.

Final Inspection & Cleanup

We perform a detailed final walkthrough, verify every element meets our standards, clean up completely, and review your new warranty coverage. Your property is left cleaner than we found it — guaranteed.

FAQ

Roof Replacement Questions

Signs you may need a replacement include: your roof is 20+ years old, there's widespread granule loss, multiple leaks or repairs are becoming frequent, you see daylight through the roof deck, or significant storm damage has occurred. We provide a thorough evaluation and will always be honest about whether a repair or full replacement is the better option.
We offer asphalt shingles (architectural and designer), cedar shakes, standing seam metal roofing, corrugated metal, stone-coated steel, and flat roof systems (Duro-Last and Mule Hide). During your consultation, we'll recommend the best material based on your home's architecture, budget, and long-term goals.
Most residential roof replacements are completed in 1–3 days for asphalt shingles. Metal roofing and cedar shake projects may take 3–5 days. Larger or more complex projects can take up to a week. Weather conditions in Minnesota can also factor into scheduling, but we plan accordingly to minimize delays.
If your roof was damaged by a covered event (hail, wind, fallen trees), your homeowners insurance typically covers the replacement cost minus your deductible. We help document all damage with drone photography and thermal imaging, work directly with your insurance adjuster, and ensure you receive fair compensation. Our detailed documentation often helps maximize approved claims.
